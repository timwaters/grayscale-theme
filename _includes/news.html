COVID UPDATE- The 4th World Congress 2020, 4th 5th & 6th September 2020

Covid-19 has made a dogged effort to stop us in our tracks but we hope, with a few nifty body swerves, to keep moving and maybe even to find new pathways and insights from it. The big point to note is that this year we will be offering a virtual Congress; there will be no physical gathering of people in Huddersfield, or anywhere else, this year.

Call for proposals

This year's theme will be: "Walk, Don't Walk"

We suspect that the period of confinement has, for most people, been a profound experience. And obliged almost all of us to alter our relationship with time and space. Enforced leisure and heightened vigilance has opened new vistas to many. People have listened to birdsong for the first time, or ventured down a strange footpath, noticed mysterious markings on an old building or simply remarked upon the quality of the air.

As restrictions loosen and we return to old patterns, much of this sensibility will be re-submerged, but surely vestiges will remain to define whatever becomes the 'new normal'. Our theme "Walk, Don't Walk" expresses that central paradox.

This year's congress will be a space for you to express your own take on that. Please send us your suggestions for events you would like to present in the programme.

We are looking for submissions which can be delivered on-line... But we don't want the Congress to become a 3 day Zoom webinar. We welcome submissions which explore other approaches, for example: the possibility of physical activity within social-distancing, film screenings, audio only...

Because the Congress will be despatialised, it can take place in any time zone of the world during those 72 hours of the long weekend, so we welcome international submissions.

There will be a two-stage selection process. An Expression of Interest followed by an invitation to submit a Detailed Proposal.

The Expression of Interest should be submitted to us (by email to philwood44@live.com) by 17.00 BST on Friday 14 June.
